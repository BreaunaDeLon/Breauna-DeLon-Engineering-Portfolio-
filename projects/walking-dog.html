Reverse Engineered Walking Dog Toy

Mechanical Systems & Kinematic Analysis Project

Team-based reverse engineering project focused on analyzing and reproducing a consumer walking toy’s internal motion mechanism through CAD modeling, motion analysis, and mechanical decomposition.

Project Overview

This project involved disassembling a commercially available walking dog toy to understand how simple mechanical components such as gears, linkages, and rotating cams combine to generate coordinated, repeatable walking motion.

The internal mechanism was measured, reconstructed, and modeled in SolidWorks to capture gear relationships, linkage geometry, and motion paths responsible for the toy’s walking behavior.
