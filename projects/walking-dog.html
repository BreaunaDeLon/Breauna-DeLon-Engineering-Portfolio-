Reverse Engineered Walking Dog Toy

Mechanical systems and kinematic analysis project focused on reverse engineering a consumer walking toy to understand coordinated motion, gear trains, and linkage-driven locomotion.

Project Overview

This project involved reverse engineering a commercially available walking dog toy to analyze how simple mechanical components can be combined to produce coordinated, repeatable walking motion.

The toy was disassembled, measured, and reconstructed in SolidWorks to model internal linkages, gear trains, and motion paths responsible for the walking behavior.
