Reverse Engineered Walking Dog Toy

Mechanical Systems & Kinematic Analysis Project

Team-based reverse engineering project focused on analyzing and reproducing a consumer toy’s internal walking mechanism through CAD modeling, motion analysis, and mechanical decomposition.

Project Overview

This project involved reverse engineering a commercially available walking dog toy to understand how simple mechanical components can be combined to produce coordinated, repeatable motion.

The internal mechanism was disassembled, measured, and reconstructed in CAD to model linkages, gear trains, and motion paths responsible for the toy’s walking behavior.

Mechanical & Kinematic Analysis

The walking motion was achieved through a motor-driven gear train connected to a multi-link leg mechanism. Rotational input from the motor was converted into oscillatory leg motion using offset linkages and pinned joints.

Special attention was given to:

  • Gear ratios and torque transmission
  • Linkage lengths and joint constraints
  • Synchronization between front and rear legs
  • Stability during ground contact phases

Team Role & Leadership

Served as team lead, coordinating task assignments, timelines, and design reviews. Responsibilities included delegating CAD modeling tasks, verifying dimensional accuracy, and ensuring consistent assembly constraints across the full model.

This role emphasized leadership, communication, and accountability while balancing technical problem-solving with team coordination.

Key Skills Demonstrated

  • Reverse engineering methodology
  • Mechanical linkage design
  • Gear train analysis
  • SolidWorks part and assembly modeling
  • Motion and interference analysis
  • Team leadership and coordination
