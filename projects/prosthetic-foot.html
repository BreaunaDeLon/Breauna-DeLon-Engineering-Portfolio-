Prosthetic Foot Design & FEA Study

Focus: Structural analysis, material selection, and low-cost design

Project Overview

This project focused on the design and structural evaluation of a low-cost prosthetic foot intended to withstand typical walking load cases while remaining manufacturable and accessible. The goal was to evaluate material performance and geometry using finite element analysis to inform design decisions.

Design and Methodology

This project focused on designing a low-cost, energy-storing prosthetic foot using SolidWorks CAD and Finite Element Analysis (FEA). The goal was to balance durability, flexibility, and affordability while maintaining safe performance under real-world loading conditions.

A static force of 980 N was applied at the hinge location to represent body weight transfer during standing and walking. Multiple load cases were evaluated to simulate upward motion (toe-off), downward motion (heel strike), and static standing.

FEA Results – Upward Walking Motion

This simulation represents toe-off during walking, where the prosthetic foot flexes upward under load. The results show controlled bending behavior with stress and strain remaining within the elastic limits of the carbon fiber foot blade.

FEA Results – Downward Walking Motion

This case simulates stepping downward, representing a worst-case bending scenario. Higher stress and displacement values were observed, but all remained below material failure limits, validating the structural integrity of the design.

FEA Results – Standing Load Case

The standing simulation represents static body weight applied to the prosthetic foot. Minimal stress, strain, and displacement were observed, confirming that the design can safely support everyday standing loads.
