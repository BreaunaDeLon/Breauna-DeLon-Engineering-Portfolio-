Project Overview

This project focused on the design and structural evaluation of a low-cost prosthetic foot intended to withstand typical walking load cases while remaining manufacturable and accessible. The goal was to evaluate material performance and geometry using finite element analysis to inform design decisions.

Key Skills Demonstrated

Mechanical & Structural Engineering

  • Mechanical design of a prosthetic foot assembly
  • Load path analysis under walking and stance conditions
  • Design for manufacturability and low-cost production
  • Geometry refinement to reduce stress concentrations

Analysis & Simulation

  • Finite Element Analysis (FEA) for stress and deformation evaluation
  • Comparison of aluminum, stainless steel, and composite materials
  • Boundary condition definition based on realistic loading scenarios
  • Interpretation of von Mises stress and displacement results

Engineering Process & Professional Skills

  • Iterative design based on simulation results
  • Failure mode consideration and design refinement
  • Technical documentation of assumptions and results
  • Independent project execution and time management

Engineering Focus & Analysis

Finite element simulations were used to evaluate stress distribution and deformation across the prosthetic foot under applied loading representative of walking conditions. Material selection was guided by strength-to-weight ratio, manufacturability, and accessibility considerations.

Results from the analysis informed geometric modifications aimed at improving structural performance while maintaining simplicity and cost-effectiveness.
